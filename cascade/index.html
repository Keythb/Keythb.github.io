1 · HOW OFTEN A CROSS SPREADS TO THE NEXT CHARTWhen the 5-minute chart makes a cross, the 15-minute chart makes the same cross within a day about 40% of the time. A 15-minute cross carries to the 1-hour chart within 3 days about 35% of the time. A 1-hour cross carries to the 4-hour chart within 10 days about 25% of the time. Most crosses never make it past their own chart — one that keeps climbing is the exception worth watching.
2 · IF RSI AGREES WITH THE CROSS, THE TREND LASTS LONGERCheck RSI at the moment a cross happens. If it already agrees with the cross — above 60 on a golden cross, below 40 on a death cross — the new trend runs noticeably longer before it reverses. If RSI disagrees, the cross usually dies early. This is the most reliable pattern in the library.
3 · DON'T BUY A GOLDEN CROSS THAT ARRIVES OVERBOUGHTOn the 15-minute to 1-hour charts, a golden cross that shows up with RSI already above 60 is usually a bad buy: on average, price is 0.45% lower 50 bars later. The trend itself often keeps going (see #2) — the problem is you're paying a stretched price for it.
4 · SMALL CHARTS GIVE WARNING TIME, NOT BETTER ODDSA cascade that already climbed one rung is not more likely to climb the next — every step stays roughly the same 40/35/25% draw. But almost every big-chart flip was preceded by the small charts flipping first: typically about 1.5 days of warning before the 1-hour flips and about 6 days before the 4-hour flips. Use the small charts as an early-warning system for staging entries and exits.
5 · A CROSS BY ITSELF IS A COIN FLIPOn any chart faster than the daily, a cross alone tells you nothing about direction — price is roughly 50/50 afterward. A cross tells you what state the market is in and when to pay attention. It is not a buy or sell signal on its own.
