1 · EACH CHART IS A GATEWhen a move flips one chart, the only race that matters is whether the next chart up flips before the current chart (the frontier) flips back. Lower charts can whipsaw and re-catch up — they don't break the cascade. Measured odds the move actually propagates forward through each gate (a fresh same-direction cross prints on the next chart inside its window): 5m→10m ~44% · 10m→15m ~56% · 15m→30m ~48% · 30m→45m ~64% · 45m→1h ~55% · 1h→2h ~42% · 2h→3h ~61% · 3h→4h ~66% · 4h→1d ~11% (2026-07-10: 1h and up now rest on a deep 1h archive back to 2016, n=39–283; 4h→1d is n=53/52 — genuinely low because a fresh daily cross is a rare event, not because the mechanic is weak). Counting a chart as "passed" whenever it is merely already in the same direction runs much higher — but that is backdrop, not forward propagation. The 1m is a tripwire (most of its crosses never even reach the 5m) and the daily is a wall — only true regime changes get through. The 1h→2h gate is the ladder's low point at ~42%, roughly even in both directions — an earlier read showing a sharp up/down split (30%/40%) was a small-sample artifact (n=30) that didn't hold up once the archive deepened to n=283.
2 · IF RSI AGREES WITH THE CROSS, THE TREND LASTS LONGERCheck RSI at the moment a cross happens. If it already agrees with the cross — above 60 on a golden cross, below 40 on a death cross — the new trend runs noticeably longer before it reverses. If RSI disagrees, the cross usually dies early. This is the most reliable pattern in the library.
3 · DON'T BUY A GOLDEN CROSS THAT ARRIVES OVERBOUGHTOn the 15-minute to 1-hour charts, a golden cross that shows up with RSI already above 60 is usually a bad buy: on average, price is 0.45% lower 50 bars later. The trend itself often keeps going (see #2) — the problem is you're paying a stretched price for it.
4 · SMALL CHARTS GIVE WARNING TIMEPassing one gate does not guarantee the next — each gate is its own draw (see #1). But almost every big-chart flip was preceded by the small charts flipping first: typically about 1.5 days of warning before the 1-hour flips and about 6 days before the 4-hour flips. Use the small charts as an early-warning system for staging entries and exits.
5 · A CROSS BY ITSELF IS A COIN FLIPOn any chart faster than the daily, a cross alone tells you nothing about direction — price is roughly 50/50 afterward. A cross tells you what state the market is in and when to pay attention. It is not a buy or sell signal on its own.
6 · BEAR MOVES PAY ON SMALL CHARTS, BULL MOVES PAY ON BIG ONESThe machinery is direction-blind (odds #1 and the RSI rule #2 are the same both ways), but the payoffs are not. On the 2h–4h charts, death crosses averaged +2.9% as shorts while golden crosses averaged −2.6% as longs — sell-offs move farther and faster (liquidations). On the daily chart it flips: golden crosses won 62.5% of the time averaging +50%, while shorting a daily death cross averaged −13% — by the time the daily crosses down, the decline is usually almost over. Smaller samples than the other findings (n=8 each side, 2018-2025); re-tested as the library grows. Deeper history exists (BTC's daily close back to 2010) but isn't blended in here — the two golden crosses it would add (2012, 2015) ran to +7,488% and +2,144% in Bitcoin's early, pre-institutional era, a different market regime that would badly distort the average rather than sharpen it.

Gate logic: a cascade starts at the most recent 5m cross and advances chart by chart. Only the frontier (the highest chart reached) matters — the cascade stays alive while the frontier holds its direction, dies if the frontier flips back before the next chart crosses, and completes at the 4h.
